About Me

Jalal Arabneydi obtained his Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from McGill University, Montreal, Canada, 2017. He is the recipient of the Best Student-Paper Award in the 53rd IEEE Conference on Decision and Control (CDC), 2014, which is a highly prestigious award given annually to one distinguished paper for its originality, clarity, and potential impact on practical applications or theoretical foundations of control.

His research interest focuses on stochastic optimization, game/control theory, reinforcement learning, decentralized and distributed systems and large-scale networks. In the past few years, he has worked on many envisioned applications such as those in health sector (e.g. control of pandemics), energy sector (e.g. integration of renewable energies), national security (e.g. cyber-physical attacks), public sector (e.g. social networks), private sector (e.g. navigation of unmanned vehicles) and financial sector (e.g. supply chain).

One of his current research interests lies in the intersection of decision making theory and artificial intelligence. The ultimate goal is to define proper mathematical tools and solution concepts in order to develop decision-making algorithms that work under imperfect information and incomplete knowledge with analytical performance guarantees. To this end, he has introduced deep structured teams/games as a class of large-scale planning algorithms.
