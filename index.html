Jalal Arabneydi

Jalal Arabneydi received his Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from McGill University, Montreal, Canada, 2017. Dr. Arabneydi has studied sequential decision making and large-scale networked control systems for past 10 years with a special interest in decentralized stochastic control, robust optimization, dynamic game as well as multi-agent reinforcement learning and artificial intelligence with applications in smart grids, swarm robotics, and economics. He is also the recipient of the Best Student-Paper Award in the 53rd IEEE Conference on Decision and Control (CDC), 2014, which is a highly prestigious award given annually to one distinguished paper for its originality, clarity, and potential impact on practical applications or theoretical foundations of control.

Email: jalal [dot] arabneydi [at] mail [dot] mcgill [dot] ca

Current research interest

My current research interest is focused on exploring the overlap between two seemingly separated fields of study, i.e., decision making theory and artificial intelligence. The ultimate goal is to define proper mathematical tools and solution concepts in order to develop large-scale decision-making algorithms that work under imperfect information and incomplete knowledge with analytical performance guarantees.
